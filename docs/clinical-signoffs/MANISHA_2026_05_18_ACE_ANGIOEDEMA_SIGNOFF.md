**DOCUMENT OVERVIEW**

This document provides clinical sign-off on the four decisions required
to implement RULE*ACE*ANGIOEDEMA. This is a P0 pilot blocker.
ACE-inhibitor angioedema causes airway obstruction in approximately 10%
of cases and can be
fatal.[\[1\]](#ghouse-j,-ahlberg-g,-andreasen-l,-et-al.-\<a-target="_blank"-href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34384552"\>association-of-variants-near-the-bradykinin-receptor-b-gene-with-angioedema-in-patients-taking-ace-inhibitors\</a\>.-journal-of-the-american-college-of-cardiology.-2021;78\(7\):696-709.-doi:10.1016/j.jacc.2021.05.054.)
The Ward 7/8 pilot cohort is predominantly African American — a
population with up to 5 times the risk of ACE-inhibitor angioedema
compared to white patients (0.72% vs. 0.31% in
ALLHAT).[\[2\]](#eskander-a,-de-almeida-jr,-irish-jc.-\<a-target="_blank"-href="https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/nejmra1811697"\>acute-upper-airway-obstruction\</a\>.-the-new-england-journal-of-medicine.-2019;381\(20\):1940-1949.-doi:10.1056/nejmra1811697.)[\[3\]](#zuraw-bl,-bernstein-ja,-lang-dm,-et-al.-\<a-target="_blank"-href="https://linkinghub.elsevier.com/retrieve/pii/s0091-6749\(13\)00523-x"\>a-focused-parameter-update:-hereditary-angioedema,-acquired-c1-inhibitor-deficiency,-and-angiotensin-converting-enzyme-inhibitor-associated-angioedema\</a\>.-the-journal-of-allergy-and-clinical-immunology.-2013;131\(6\):1491-3.-doi:10.1016/j.jaci.2013.03.034.)

Original draft:

"You reported swelling of your face, lips, or tongue. This needs urgent
medical attention. Call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room now."

Approved revision:

**QUESTION 2 — SYMPTOM TRIGGER**

Decision: ADD TWO NEW BUTTONS, not one combined checkbox.

Engineering recommended option (d): a single "Sudden swelling of face,
lips, or tongue" checkbox. This is close but insufficient. Throat
tightness / difficulty swallowing is a separate anatomical symptom that
indicates laryngeal involvement — the specific mechanism that kills
patients.[\[1\]](#ghouse-j,-ahlberg-g,-andreasen-l,-et-al.-\<a-target="_blank"-href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34384552"\>association-of-variants-near-the-bradykinin-receptor-b-gene-with-angioedema-in-patients-taking-ace-inhibitors\</a\>.-journal-of-the-american-college-of-cardiology.-2021;78\(7\):696-709.-doi:10.1016/j.jacc.2021.05.054.)[\[4\]](#moellman-jj,-bernstein-ja,-lindsell-c,-et-al.-\<a-target="_blank"-href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/pmc4100605/"\>a-consensus-parameter-for-the-evaluation-and-management-of-angioedema-in-the-emergency-department\</a\>.-academic-emergency-medicine-:-official-journal-of-the-society-for-academic-emergency-medicine.-2014;21\(4\):469-84.-doi:10.1111/acem.12341.)

part of the same emergency. Splitting into two buttons captures both
presentations.

**QUESTION 3 — TIER + DISPATCH**

Decision: CONFIRM Tier 1 emergency with proposed dispatch chain. ADD
compressed escalation timeline.

The proposed dispatch is correct:

\- Patient: full-screen red emergency page + 911 button

\- Primary provider: push + email

**Enhancement — compressed escalation (faster than standard):**

The standard escalation ladder runs T+0 → T+4h → T+8h → T+24h → T+48h.
Angioedema requires a faster escalation because airway obstruction can
progress rapidly — attacks last 48–72 hours and require hospital
admission in most cases, with intubation needed in approximately
10%.[\[1\]](#ghouse-j,-ahlberg-g,-andreasen-l,-et-al.-\<a-target="_blank"-href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/34384552"\>association-of-variants-near-the-bradykinin-receptor-b-gene-with-angioedema-in-patients-taking-ace-inhibitors\</a\>.-journal-of-the-american-college-of-cardiology.-2021;78\(7\):696-709.-doi:10.1016/j.jacc.2021.05.054.)[\[4\]](#moellman-jj,-bernstein-ja,-lindsell-c,-et-al.-\<a-target="_blank"-href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/pmc4100605/"\>a-consensus-parameter-for-the-evaluation-and-management-of-angioedema-in-the-emergency-department\</a\>.-academic-emergency-medicine-:-official-journal-of-the-society-for-academic-emergency-medicine.-2014;21\(4\):469-84.-doi:10.1111/acem.12341.)[\[9\]](#pines-jm,-poarch-k,-hughes-s.-\<a-target="_blank"-href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33218838"\>recognition-and-differential-diagnosis-of-hereditary-angioedema-in-the-emergency-department\</a\>.-the-journal-of-emergency-medicine.-2021;60\(1\):35-43.-doi:10.1016/j.jemermed.2020.09.044.)

\`\`\`

ANGIOEDEMA-SPECIFIC ESCALATION:

T+0: Patient sees emergency screen + 911 CTA

T+0: Primary provider receives push + email

T+0: Caregiver receives push (if enabled)

T+15m: If primary provider has NOT acknowledged →

backup provider receives push + email

T+1h: If NEITHER provider has acknowledged →

medical director receives push + email

\+ Healplace ops flagged

T+4h: If NO provider has acknowledged →

incident report auto-generated

\`\`\`

Why 15 minutes (not 4 hours) to backup: Angioedema can progress from
mild lip swelling to complete airway obstruction within hours. The
standard 4-hour backup window is designed for BP alerts where the
patient is hemodynamically stable. An airway emergency requires a
fundamentally different response timeline.

**Non-dismissible:** Like all Tier 1 alerts, this alert cannot be
dismissed without a documented resolution rationale in the 15-field
Joint Commission audit trail.

**QUESTION 4 — ACE-INHIBITOR PROFILE REQUIRED, OR ANY PATIENT?**

Decision: FIRE TIER 1 FOR ALL PATIENTS regardless of medication profile.
Branch the physician message based on whether the patient is on an ACE
inhibitor, ARB, or neither.

This is the most important clinical decision in this document.

**Why the emergency fires for ALL patients:** Sudden swelling of the
face, lips, or tongue is a potential airway emergency regardless of
cause. The Society for Academic Emergency Medicine consensus parameter
explicitly states: "if the cause of angioedema is not apparent, initial
standard of care treatment should include epinephrine, H1 and H2
antagonists and oral
corticosteroids".[\[6\]](#long-bj,-koyfman-a,-gottlieb-m.-\<a-target="_blank"-href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31316698"\>evaluation-and-management-of-angioedema-in-the-emergency-department\</a\>.-the-western-journal-of-emergency-medicine.-2019;20\(4\):587-600.-doi:10.5811/westjem.2019.5.42650.)

C1-inhibitor deficiency, allergic reactions, or idiopathic causes — all
of which can progress to airway
obstruction.[\[4\]](#moellman-jj,-bernstein-ja,-lindsell-c,-et-al.-\<a-target="_blank"-href="https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/pmc4100605/"\>a-consensus-parameter-for-the-evaluation-and-management-of-angioedema-in-the-emergency-department\</a\>.-academic-emergency-medicine-:-official-journal-of-the-society-for-academic-emergency-medicine.-2014;21\(4\):469-84.-doi:10.1111/acem.12341.)[\[6\]](#long-bj,-koyfman-a,-gottlieb-m.-\<a-target="_blank"-href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31316698"\>evaluation-and-management-of-angioedema-in-the-emergency-department\</a\>.-the-western-journal-of-emergency-medicine.-2019;20\(4\):587-600.-doi:10.5811/westjem.2019.5.42650.)[\[7\]](#montinaro-v,-cicardi-m.-\<a-target="_blank"-href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31835086"\>ace-inhibitor-mediated-angioedema\</a\>.-international-immunopharmacology.-2020;78:106081.-doi:10.1016/j.intimp.2019.106081.)[\[8\]](#depetri-f,-tedeschi-a,-cugno-m.-\<a-target="_blank"-href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30220453"\>angioedema-and-emergency-medicine:-from-pathophysiology-to-diagnosis-and-treatment\</a\>.-european-journal-of-internal-medicine.-2019;59:8-13.-doi:10.1016/j.ejim.2018.09.004.)
